Benefits

The potential health benefits associated with drinking coffee include protecting against type 2 diabetes, Parkinson's disease, liver disease, liver cancer, and promoting a healthy heart.

Nutrition

TRegular black coffee (without milk or cream) has a very low calorie count. A typical cup of black coffee only contains around 2 calories.

Risk

Drinking too much coffee can result in some very unpleasant adverse effects. According to a study by researchers at the University of Oklahoma, "caffeine can cause anxiety symptoms in normal individuals, especially in vulnerable patients, like those with pre-existing anxiety disorders.


AFFOGATO COFFEE

Affo what? Affogato coffee is mainly found in Italy and serves as a drink for the hot summer days. Often times Affogato is also ordered as a dessert.It’s rather simple to make it. Scoop one or two spoons of vanilla ice-cream into a glas tumbler and pour a single or double espresso shot over the ice-cream. Surprise your guests and serve after lunch!

AMERICANO COFFEE

Americano coffee, or a long black coffee is 2rd’s of water and 1rd of espresso shot. Americano is an espresso drink using water instead of milk. Commonly thought, the name derives from “American Coffee” during World War II when Americans added water to their espresso to create a larger coffee that they were accustomed to back home. Simply boil water (around 200ml or 6 oz), let it rest for a minute (don’t use the water while it’s boiling) and pour in a cup or mug. Then prepare 1 shot of espresso (75ml / 2 oz or more if you like it stronger) and pour on top of the hot water.

CAPPUCCINO COFFEE

Cappuccino is thought to be named after monks as their robes resembled the color of the coffee. A cappuccino is similar to a Café Latte however the main difference is that it is rather served in a cup instead of a glas tumbler and it usually has foam and chocolate powder on top. Prepare 1 shot of espresso and pour it into a cup. Then add steamed milk (5 oz or 150ml) and foam from the steamed milk on top. Finish it off by sprinkling chocolate powder on top. Voilá!

CAFÉ LATTE

Café Latte or sometimes simply referred to as “latte” derives from the italian meaning “milk coffee”. Most commonly a latte also served with “latte art” in where the barista will try to create a pattern while pouring the milk on top of the espresso. Often this will be a flower or a heart shape. Latte is also a popular choice to drink with soy or other milk types.To prepare a latte use a cup or tumbler and pour 1 espresso shot in. Then steam milk and pour on top (5 oz or 150ml). Finish off by putting some micro foam on top with a spoon.

DOUBLE ESPRESSO

A double espresso is a strong coffee simply consisting of two espresso shots (5 oz or 150ml). Usually served in a cup, this coffee drink is often served with cookies. Many people also enjoy a double espresso or also referred to as “doppio” with a spoon of brown sugar to soften the bitter taste.

ESPRESSO

The espresso serves as the foundation to many coffee drinks but for the purpose of this post, a single espresso as the name suggest consists of 1 shot (2 oz or 75ml) of espresso. Espresso is usually consumed as a standalone coffee drink after a meal.

IRISH COFFEE

For those cold winter months you might want to enjoy an Irish coffee. Irish coffee as you may have guessed includes Irish whiskey. It is usually served in a special Irish coffee whiskey glas, very sweet and recommended to be prepared with actual Irish whiskey. Pre-heat the coffee glasses by pouring boiling water into them. Remove the water, place 1-2 sugar cubes in them and pour hot coffee over it (about 3/4 full). Stir the blend to dissolve the sugar and pour 1 shot (1 oz or 30 ml) of Irish whiskey on top. To finish off add 3 tablespoons of whipped cream.

PICCOLO LATTE

Piccolo comes from the Italian and stands for “short”. Simply put a Piccolo latte is a shorter version of the Café latte and has usually 1 shot of espresso (2oz or 150ml) and steamed milk (150ml or 2 oz) poured on top. To finish off you may add micro foam on top.

MACCHIATO COFFEE

Macchiato coffee is usually 1 shot of espresso topped off with milk foam. Many countries also serve a large macchiato which is nothing more than a double espresso shot with milk foam on top. Macchiato is mostly drunk by espresso lovers who want a bit of a sweeter taste. The milk foam helps to soften the bitter espresso taste.

MELANGE COFFEE

Melange coffee is usually served in Austria and similar to a Cappuccino (without the chocolate powder on top). The main difference is often said to be the lighter roast of beans used when compared to a Cappuccino.

MOCA COFFEE

A moca coffee is usually a blend of cappuccino and a hot chocolate. It serves ideally as a dessert or light coffee drink for younger adults. Pour 1 shot of espresso into a cup or short latte glass and add 1 spoon of chocolate or cocoa powder in it and stir. Add steamed milk (5 oz or 150 ml) and top off with micro foam. To finish off sprinkle chocolate or cocoa powder on top

RISTRETTO COFFEE

A Ristretto coffee is a coffee made with the same amount of coffee grounds as a espresso but half the amount of water. If you are using an automatic machine or capsule machine you may set this on your machine. If you are using a manual coffee maker you may simply use half of the water you normally use. A Ristretto is usually very strong and not for everyone.

VIENNESE COFFEE

Viennese coffee is a very strong coffee mixed with whipped cream and topped off with chocolate powder or chocolate sauce. Prepare two shots of espresso and pour into a cup or glass tumbler. Fill with whipped cream until the glass is full and top off with chocolate powder.
